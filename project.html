The Project

Vakyasetu is an assistive communication system designed to help individuals with paralysis express intent through minimal physical interaction. The project focuses on translating simple hand-based touch inputs into meaningful communication using a tightly integrated hardware and software approach.

The Problem

For many paralysed individuals, the loss of speech or motor control creates a severe communication barrier. Existing solutions often rely on expensive equipment, invasive technologies, or complex calibration processes that are difficult to access, maintain, or scale.

In low-resource environments, these limitations become even more critical, leaving individuals dependent on others for even basic communication.

The Core Idea

Vakyasetu approaches the problem from a different direction. Instead of requiring full motion or speech, it leverages small, intentional hand interactions detected through touch sensors. These interactions are interpreted by software logic that maps signals to predefined meanings.

The system is built to work with minimal effort from the user, prioritizing comfort, simplicity, and clarity.

What Vakyasetu Does

The system captures touch-based input, processes the signal using structured algorithms, and converts the result into understandable output such as words, phrases, or commands. This allows users to communicate without relying on speech or full limb movement.

The design emphasizes reliability and repeatability, ensuring that small actions lead to consistent outcomes.

Why This Approach Matters

Vakyasetu is not designed as a closed or rigid solution. It is modular, adaptable, and learning-oriented. Both the hardware and software components are structured to evolve based on user needs and future research.

By focusing on affordability, transparency, and ease of use, the project aims to lower the entry barrier for assistive communication technology.
