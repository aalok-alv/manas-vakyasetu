The Team

Vakyasetu is developed under the MANAS initiative, a collaborative effort focused on creating human-centered assistive technologies. The team operates with a research-first mindset, emphasizing learning, experimentation, and responsible engineering.

Team Structure

The project is built through interdisciplinary collaboration. Each member contributes across multiple domains while maintaining clear responsibility areas to ensure system integrity and progress.

Core contribution areas include hardware development, software engineering, system integration, documentation, and research analysis.

The MANAS Initiative

MANAS is a learning-driven initiative aimed at developing accessible and ethical technology solutions. Projects under MANAS focus on real-world problems, especially those affecting individuals with limited access to advanced technological resources.

Vakyasetu represents one such effort, combining practical engineering with social responsibility.

Vision

The long-term vision of Vakyasetu is to contribute toward a future where communication barriers caused by physical limitations can be reduced through simple, affordable, and adaptable technology.

The project does not aim to replace existing medical or therapeutic solutions, but to complement them by providing additional tools for expression and interaction.

Roadmap

Short-Term: Improve sensor reliability, refine signal interpretation, and conduct controlled user testing.

Mid-Term: Expand gesture vocabulary, enhance software adaptability, and improve system modularity.

Long-Term: Collaborate with researchers and institutions, explore integration with other assistive systems, and study scalability options.

Ethical Commitment

Vakyasetu is built with a strong ethical foundation. User dignity, transparency, and non-invasive assistance guide every design decision.

The team remains committed to responsible development, ensuring that technology empowers users without introducing dependency or unnecessary complexity.
